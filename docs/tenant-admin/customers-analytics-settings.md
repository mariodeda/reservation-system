# Customers, Analytics, And Settings

Customers, analytics, and settings help managers understand guest history,
service performance, and tenant-local preferences. These pages are less urgent
than Reservations during service, but they are important for good operations.

## Clients & Statistics Navigation

The header groups Customers and Analytics under `Clients & Statistics`.

Use:

- Customers when you need guest information or history.
- Analytics when you need performance trends.

The dropdown should close when staff click outside it. On smaller screens, the
UI may use a compact selector instead of a desktop dropdown.

## Customers Page

The Customers page helps staff find guest records.

Search by:

- Name.
- Email.
- Phone.

Use Customers to:

- Identify returning guests.
- Confirm contact details.
- Review upcoming reservations.
- Review past visits.
- Find guests with email delivery problems.
- Check notes that may affect service.

## Customer Records

Customer records are only useful when contact data is consistent. Staff should:

- Enter names consistently.
- Use accurate phone numbers.
- Correct email typos when guests provide updates.
- Avoid creating duplicate records when possible.
- Keep notes service-relevant.

Do not store unrelated sensitive personal information in notes.

## Email Reachability And Customers

If a reservation shows an email warning, the customer record may need cleanup.
Staff should call the guest and confirm the correct email. This improves future
confirmation and review request delivery.

## Analytics Page

Analytics summarizes restaurant performance over a selected period.

Common metrics include:

- Reservation count.
- Covers.
- Guest count.
- Service breakdown.
- Source breakdown.
- No-show indicators.
- Lead-time trends.
- Customer trends.

Analytics are only as accurate as the operational data. If staff forget to mark
completed, cancelled, or no-show reservations, reports will be misleading.

## How Managers Should Use Analytics

Use analytics to answer questions like:

- Which services are busiest?
- Which days are under-booked?
- Are no-shows increasing?
- Are guests booking too late?
- Does lead time need adjustment?
- Are table durations realistic?
- Are large parties common enough to adjust policy?
- Should staffing change for a specific service?

Analytics should guide operational decisions, not replace manager judgement.

## Settings Page

Tenant settings are for local staff preferences and password management.

Staff may see:

- Local account settings.
- Staff password change.
- Tenant-level operational preferences.

Staff should not see:

- SMTP credentials.
- Public tenant key.
- Allowed origins.
- Domains.
- Platform logs.
- Platform email logs.
- Global email flow switches.

Those are platform-admin responsibilities.

## Password Change

Staff can change the tenant admin password from settings when allowed.

Good practice:

- Use a strong password.
- Do not share passwords in plain chat.
- Change passwords after staff turnover.
- Sign out on shared devices.

Password changes may be disabled during platform impersonation. If staff cannot
change the password, ask platform support whether an operator is currently
supporting the tenant or whether a platform reset is needed.

## Data Quality Checklist

For better customers and analytics:

1. Use accurate guest names.
2. Require at least one reliable contact method.
3. Fix obvious email typos.
4. Mark no-shows.
5. Mark completed visits.
6. Mark cancellations rather than deleting normal cancellations.
7. Keep notes concise and operational.
8. Avoid duplicate customer records.

## Common Questions

### Why can analytics change after service?

Statuses may be updated after guests leave. Analytics reflect the latest saved
data.

### Why does a customer appear twice?

The guest may have booked with different phone or email details. Use consistent
contact details going forward.

### Can staff export customer data?

Use only the tools available in the tenant UI. If a special export is needed,
ask platform support and follow the restaurant's privacy process.

### Why can staff not change email templates?

Email templates and flow policy are platform-controlled to protect sender
identity, delivery quality, and configuration safety.
